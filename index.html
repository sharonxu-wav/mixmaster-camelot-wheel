The Core Rules of Harmonic Mixing

The Camelot Wheel is a circular key signature map designed by Mark Davis. Keys are numbered 1 through 12, like hours on a clock, with a letter suffix of A (Minor scale) or B (Major scale).

  • Perfect Match (Same Key): e.g. 8A → 8A. Zero transition risk. 100% harmonically compatible.
  • Relative Shift (A ↔ B): e.g. 8A (A minor) → 8B (C major). Smooth transition; shifts emotional mood since both keys share the same notes.
  • Step Neighbor (+/- 1 number): e.g. 8A → 9A (clockwise: raises energy) or 8A → 7A (counter-clockwise: settles energy).

Advanced Pro Mixing Techniques

Beyond standard neighboring moves, pro DJs use intentional jumps across the wheel to construct dynamic set energy:

  • Energy Boost (+2 steps): e.g. 8A → 10A. Pitch lifts a whole step. A noticeable burst of tension and acceleration.
  • Semitone Lift (+7 steps): e.g. 8A → 3A. Lifts the key exactly one half-step. Maximum energy builder, perfect for beat-drops.
  • Diagonal Transition: e.g. 8A → 9B. Moves one number AND flips major/minor. A warm, complex emotional flow.
